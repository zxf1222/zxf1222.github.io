Natural Disasters

Japan is known for experiencing many natural disasters due to its unique geography.
The nation is located on the Pacific Ring of Fire.
This specific region is known for its high seismic and volcanic activity.
People living in Japan may also experience typhoons, floods, and landslides.

  • Major Earthquakes: Hanshin-Awaji (1995), East Japan (2011)
  • Major Volcanic Eruptions: Mount Usu (2000), Mount Ontake (2014)
  • Major Typhoons: Typhoon Hagibis (2019), Typhoon Jebi (2018)

Japan is a homogeneous society with a rich cultural heritage.
It is known for its technological advancements and has a strong economy.

Religion

Religion in Japan is a mix of Shintoism, Buddhism, and other traditions.
About 70.5% of the population practices Shintoism, 67.2% practices Buddhism.
Also 1.5% practices Christianity. Another 5.9% follow other religions or no religion.

Transportation

Japan is one of the msot advanced countries when it comes to transportation.

General Transportation Information:

  • Number of Airports: 175
  • Total Railways: 27,311 kilometers
  • Total Roadways: 1,218,772 kilometers
  • Major seaports: Tokyo, Yokohama, Kobe, Osaka, Nagoya

Air Transportation Information:

Japan has a well-developed network of airports, with 175 total airports as of 2021.
The largest airports in Japan are Tokyo Haneda and Narita International Airport.
These airports include domestic and international flights.
They serve many direct flights to major cities around the world.

Rail Transportation Information:

Japan is famous for its efficient and extensive rail network.
This includes both high-speed bullet trains and local commuter trains.
The Shinkansen is also called 'bullet' train as it is known for speed.
It connects cities around Japan.
The Japan Rail Pass is a popular option for tourists!
