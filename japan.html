Japan Fact Sheet

Introduction

Japan is an island country located in East Asia. It consists of four main islands and many smaller islands and islets. With a population of over 126 million people, Japan is the world's eleventh most populous country.

Natural Disasters

Japan is known for experiencing many natural disasters due to its unique geography. It is located on the Pacific Ring of Fire, which is known for its high seismic and volcanic activity. Japan also experiences typhoons, floods, and landslides.

  • Major Earthquakes: Great Hanshin-Awaji Earthquake (1995), Great East Japan Earthquake (2011)
  • Major Volcanic Eruptions: Mount Usu (2000), Mount Ontake (2014)
  • Major Typhoons: Typhoon Hagibis (2019), Typhoon Jebi (2018)

Japan is a homogeneous society with a rich cultural heritage. It is known for its technological advancements and has a strong economy.

Religion

Religion in Japan is a mix of Shintoism, Buddhism, and other traditions. About 70.5% of the population practices Shintoism, 67.2% practices Buddhism, and 1.5% practices Christianity. Another 5.9% follow other religions or no religion.

Transportation

Japan has one of the most advanced transportation systems in the world.

General Transportation Information:

  • Number of Airports: 175
  • Total Railways: 27,311 kilometers
  • Total Roadways: 1,218,772 kilometers
  • Major seaports: Tokyo, Yokohama, Kobe, Osaka, Nagoya, Yokkaichi

Air Transportation Information:

Japan has a well-developed network of airports, with 175 total airports as of 2021. The largest airports in Japan are Tokyo Haneda Airport, Narita International Airport, and Kansai International Airport. These airports serve both domestic and international flights, with many direct flights to major cities around the world.

Rail Transportation Information:

Japan is famous for its efficient and extensive rail network, which includes both high-speed bullet trains and local commuter trains. The Shinkansen, or bullet train, connects major cities in Japan and is known for its speed, safety, and punctuality. The Japan Rail Pass is a popular option for visitors to Japan, allowing unlimited travel on Japan Rail trains for a set period of time.
